About Landscape

A landscape is the noticeable functions of an area of land, its landforms, and exactly how they integrate with all-natural or human-made functions, commonly taken into consideration in terms of their visual charm. A landscape includes the physical elements of geophysically specified landforms such as mountains, hills, water bodies such as rivers, lakes, ponds and the sea, living elements of land cover consisting of native plant life, human components including different forms of land use, buildings, and structures, and transitory aspects such as lights and weather conditions. Combining both their physical origins and the cultural overlay of human existence, usually developed over centuries, landscapes show a living synthesis of people and place that is important to regional and nationwide identity. The character of a landscape aids define the self-image of individuals that occupy it and a sense of place that separates one region from various other regions. It is the dynamic backdrop to individuals's lives. Landscape can be as varied as farmland, a landscape park or wild. The Earth has a vast series of landscapes including the icy landscapes of polar regions, mountainous landscapes, large arid desert landscapes, islands, and coastal landscapes, densely forested or wooded landscapes consisting of past boreal forests and tropical rainforests and agricultural landscapes of temperate and exotic regions. The activity of customizing the visible functions of an area of land is described as landscaping.

Frequently Asked Questions

What landscaping features are low-maintenance but visually appealing?

Features like decorative gravel, native plants, mulch beds, and stone walkways require less maintenance and hold up well in our seasonal climate.

What is the best time to start a landscaping project in Winnipeg?

Spring is the ideal time to begin a landscaping project in Winnipeg, typically late April to early May, once the ground has thawed and the risk of frost has passed.

Do I need a permit for landscaping projects in Winnipeg?

Some major landscape changes like grading or adding large structures may require permits. We can help advise and manage all necessary documentation where applicable.

How often should I water new sod?

New sod should be watered daily for the first week, then every other day for the second. After that, you can transition to a regular lawn care schedule depending on rainfall.
