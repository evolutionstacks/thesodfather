Landscape design refers to any type of activity that customizes the visible functions of a location of land, consisting of the following: Living aspects, such as vegetation or fauna; or what is frequently called horticulture, the art and craft of expanding plants with a goal of developing a charm within the landscape. All-natural abiotic aspects, such as landforms, surface shape and elevation, or bodies of water. Abstract components, such as the climate and lighting conditions. Landscaping needs a specific understanding of horticulture and artistic design, but is not limited to plants and cultivation. Shaping land to improve functionality (patio, pathways, fish ponds, water features) are additionally instances of landscaping being used. When meant as simply an aesthetic change, the term Decorative Landscape design is utilized.
